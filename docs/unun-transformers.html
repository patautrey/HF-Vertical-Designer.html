Transformer & UNUN Selection Guide

This guide explains how to choose the correct transformer or UNUN for your vertical antenna. Transformer selection is one of the most important decisions in portable HF operation — the wrong ratio can cause high SWR, wasted power, and overheated cores.

1. What Transformers Do

Transformers and UNUNs convert the antenna’s feedpoint impedance to something closer to 50 Ω. Each antenna type produces a different impedance range, so choosing the correct ratio is essential.

Rule: Match the transformer ratio to the expected feedpoint impedance of the antenna length and type.

2. Common Transformer Ratios

Ratio Impedance Range Used For Notes
1:1 Balun 50–75 Ω Vertical dipoles, OCF vertical dipoles Balanced antennas only
4:1 UNUN 150–600 Ω 17.5 ft, 20.5 ft, 25 ft Rybakov family Most stable for non-resonant verticals
9:1 UNUN 300–1200 Ω 29 ft, 41 ft, helical 33 ft random wires Best for long wires and random lengths
49:1 UNUN 2000–5000 Ω 20m EFHW, 40m EFHW Standard EFHW transformer
64:1 UNUN 2500–6000 Ω 15m EFHW Lower SWR than 49:1 on 15m

3. When to Use Each Transformer

3.1 1:1 Balun

Use for antennas that are naturally balanced:

  • Vertical dipoles
  • OCF vertical dipoles
  • Two-element vertical Yagis (driven element)

3.2 4:1 UNUN

Best for the Rybakov family:

  • 17.5 ft
  • 20.5 ft
  • 25 ft

These lengths avoid resonance and stay in the 200–600 Ω range.

3.3 9:1 UNUN

Best for long or random wires:

  • 29 ft
  • 41 ft
  • Helical 33 ft
Important: A 29 ft wire with a 49:1 transformer will produce extreme SWR and poor performance.

3.4 49:1 UNUN

Standard EFHW transformer for:

  • 20m EFHW (33 ft)
  • 40m EFHW (65 ft)

3.5 64:1 UNUN

Best for 15m EFHW:

  • Lower SWR
  • Less reflected power
  • Reduced transformer heating

4. Transformer Behavior by Antenna Length

Length Best Transformer Reason
17.5 ft 4:1 Moderate impedance, non-resonant
20.5 ft 4:1 5/8-wave on 10m, stable on others
25 ft 4:1 Classic Rybakov
29 ft 9:1 Random-wire behavior
41 ft 9:1 Long-wire impedance
33 ft (EFHW) 49:1 Half-wave on 20m
21 ft 11 in (EFHW) 64:1 Half-wave on 15m

5. Transformer Warnings

⚠️ Using the wrong transformer ratio can cause:
  • High SWR
  • Overheated cores
  • Reduced ERP
  • RF on the coax shield
  • Unstable tuner behavior
⚠️ Never use a 49:1 on a random wire.
It is designed for EFHW antennas only.
